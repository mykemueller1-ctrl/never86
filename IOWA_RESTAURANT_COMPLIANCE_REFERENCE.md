## 1. Food Safety & Health Inspection Laws

**Governing Authority:** Iowa Department of Inspections, Appeals, & Licensing (DIAL)
**Key Laws:** Iowa Code Chapter 137F, Iowa Administrative Code Chapters 481-30 and 481-31
**Standard:** 2017 FDA Food Code (adopted with Iowa modifications)

### Critical Temperature Requirements

| Item | Temperature | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Cold Holding (TCS foods) | 41°F (5°C) or below | Cheese, sliced tomatoes, meats |
| Hot Holding | 135°F (57°C) or above | All hot TCS foods |
| Poultry Cooking | 165°F (74°C) | Less than 1 second |
| Ground Meats (sausage) | 155°F (68°C) | 17 seconds |
| Pork / Whole Muscle | 145°F (63°C) | 15 seconds |
| Cooling Step 1 | 135°F → 70°F | Within 2 hours |
| Cooling Step 2 | 70°F → 41°F | Within 4 additional hours |
| Handwashing Water | 100°F minimum | |
| Sanitizing Water | 171°F minimum | |

### Certification Requirements
- **Certified Food Protection Manager (CFPM)** required — at least one per establishment
- Accredited programs: ServSafe (Iowa Restaurant Association), 360training
- General food handler training recommended for all staff

### Violation Penalties
- **Civil penalty:** Up to $1,000 per day
- **Risk Factor violations:** Must be corrected within 10 days
- **Good Retail Practice violations:** 90-day correction period

### Public Inspection Database
- Search at: https://iowa.safefoodinspection.com/Inspection/PublicInspectionSearch.aspx
- Search by county (Buena Vista County) to see local inspection history

## 2. Liquor License & Alcohol Service

**Governing Authority:** Iowa Department of Revenue — Alcohol & Tax Operations Division
**Portal:** GovConnectIowa (online application)

### License Requirements
- Apply at least **45 days** in advance
- Local authority approval needed **15 days** prior to effective date
- Minimum **25-patron seating** required for on-premises consumption
- Restroom required
- Applicant must demonstrate good moral character, tax compliance, clean criminal record
- Felony conviction within 5 years = automatic disqualifier

### Dramshop Liability (Iowa Code §123.92)
- Licensees liable for damages caused by intoxicated patrons served while visibly intoxicated
- **Noneconomic damages cap: $250,000** (unless substantial permanent loss)
- Extends to injuries/damages sustained by third parties

### Key Alcohol Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Minor Sale Fine | $1,500 |
| Business Minor Sale Penalty | $500 |
| Server Training | I-PACT recommended |
| 16-17 Year Olds Serving | Allowed in restaurants (not bars) with written parental consent + 2 adult employees present |

## 3. Iowa Labor Laws for Restaurants

### Wage Requirements

| Category | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| **Minimum Wage** | $7.25/hr (same as federal) |
| **Tipped Minimum Wage** | $4.35/hr |
| **Tip Credit** | $2.90/hr (40%) |
| **Tipped Employee Threshold** | $30+/month in tips |
| **Overtime Threshold** | 40 hours/week |
| **Overtime Rate** | 1.5x full minimum wage |

### Key Rules
- **Overtime for tipped employees** calculated on full $7.25, not $4.35
- **No mandatory breaks** for adult employees in Iowa
- **Minors under 16:** 30-minute break required for 5+ hour shifts
- **Tip pooling:** Allowed, but if tip credit taken, pool can only include tipped employees
- **At-will employment** state — either party can terminate at any time for any legal reason
- **Workers' comp** required for employers with 1+ employees

### Youth Employment (Under 18)
- 16-17 year olds can serve alcohol in restaurants with parental consent
- Must have 2 adult employees present
- Sexual harassment training required for minor employees

## 4. Workers' Compensation & Safety

### Requirements
- **Coverage:** Mandatory for 1+ employees
- **Employee injury report deadline:** 90 days
- **Employer injury report deadline:** 4 days (First Report of Injury)
- **Benefit claims statute of limitations:** 2 years from injury
- **Additional benefits:** 3 years from last payment
- **Weekly disability cap:** 80% of earnings, max $1,864/week

### OSHA Restaurant Hazards
- Slips/falls from wet or greasy floors
- Burns from hot surfaces and liquids
- Cuts from knives and slicers
- Ergonomic injuries from repetitive tasks

### Required Workplace Posters (Iowa + Federal)
- Minimum poster size: 8.5" x 14"
- Available at: https://workforce.iowa.gov/employers/resources/posters
- New hire reporting: Iowa Centralized Employee Registry (CER) within 15 days

## 8. Licensing & Plan Review

### License Fees
- Annual fee: **$150-$400** (based on gross sales)
- New establishments pay maximum fee
- Application: Submit **30 days** before opening
- Renewal window: Up to **90 days** before expiration

### Plan Review Requirements
- Required before any construction or remodeling
- Must include: proposed menu + to-scale layout
- Processing time: **3-4 weeks**
- Menu changes may trigger new plan review

### Grease Trap Requirements
- Required for grease-laden waste fixtures
- 50 GPM flow-through = 100 lbs grease retention
- Food waste disposers must NOT connect to grease interceptor

### Allergen Labeling
- Not explicitly required on menus by Iowa law
- **Best practice:** Create allergen communication system for staff
- Iowa cottage food laws: https://iowakitchenconnect.com/label
